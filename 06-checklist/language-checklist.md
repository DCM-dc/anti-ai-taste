# 06-checklist / Language

> Quick-reference leaf-node checklist. Run after producing text output.
> This catches defaults; the principle layer (`03-language/`) prevents them.

---

## Vocabulary

- [ ] Count signature words per 500 words. If > 3, rewrite.
- [ ] English signature words scanned for: delve, leverage, utilize, foster, harness, catalyze, unlock, unleash, elevate, revolutionize, amplify, illuminate, elucidate, supercharge, resonate, navigate, embrace, embark, underscore, meticulous, pivotal, robust, intricate, nuanced, seamless, vibrant, unparalleled, comprehensive, tapestry, landscape, realm, paradigm.
- [ ] Chinese signature words scanned for: 至关重要, 综上所述, 由此可见, 不难看出, 值得注意的是, 与此同时, 在当下时代, 赋能, 迭代, 多措并举, 根植于, 奠定基础, 标志着……的转变, 时代的转折点, 不断变化的格局, 具有重要意义, 行稳致远, 双向奔赴, 守正创新, 有机统一, 相辅相成.
- [ ] Marketing 4-pack scanned for: "Build the future of," "Supercharge your," "Harness the power of," "Empower your team."
- [ ] Each adjective gives specific information ("seamless" → no; "0-config, 3-second startup" → yes).
- [ ] Each abstract verb replaced with concrete action ("leverage" → "fine-tune Llama-3 on commit history").
- [ ] Proper nouns, numbers, dates: at least 3 per 500 words.
- [ ] Could this paragraph appear in 1000 other documents? If yes, add specificity.

## Syntax

- [ ] Count of "not X, but Y" structures: ≤ 1 per piece.
- [ ] Count of three-part parallelism: ≤ 1 per piece.
- [ ] Sentence openings varied (no 3+ in a row starting the same way).
- [ ] Sentence length ratio (longest / shortest) ≥ 3 in each paragraph.
- [ ] At least one sentence < 8 words per 200 words.
- [ ] At least one sentence > 30 words per 200 words.
- [ ] Passive constructions: ≤ 5 per 500 words.
- [ ] No hedge stacks (2+ hedges in one sentence).
- [ ] Each syntactic flourish (parallelism, contrast, metaphor) carries information. If not, delete.

## Rhythm

- [ ] Sentence-length range in sample paragraph: max/min ratio ≥ 3.
- [ ] Paragraph-length range in sample section: at least one 1-sentence paragraph per 1000 words.
- [ ] At least 1 fragment per 500 words ("Died." "Three of them." "Probably.").
- [ ] At least 1 question per 500 words (and not all immediately answered).
- [ ] Asides (em-dashes, parentheticals) used: at least 1 per 500 words.
- [ ] Transition words (furthermore, moreover, additionally): ≤ 2 per 500 words.
- [ ] Punctuation variety: at least 4 of {comma, period, em-dash, colon, semicolon, question mark, parentheses} used per 500 words.
- [ ] Read aloud: prose has a beat. If metronomic, vary.

## Register

- [ ] Register shifts: technical / casual / blunt appear where appropriate (not all one register).
- [ ] First person used where appropriate ("I" / "we" with specific experience).
- [ ] No "it depends" without specifying what it depends on.
- [ ] No equal-weight pros/cons without stating which to use when.
- [ ] Hedging specific ("might matter more at 200 people"), not generic ("may or may not be relevant").

## Information density

- [ ] Every claim grounded in: specific number, named source, or first-person experience.
- [ ] No "studies show" without naming the study.
- [ ] No "research indicates" without naming the research.
- [ ] No "many users report" without naming the survey.
- [ ] No "in recent years" without specifying years.

## Final

- [ ] Read aloud. Mark any sentence that sounds "smart but empty." Replace with content.
- [ ] Could this paragraph appear in 1000 other documents? If yes, add specificity.
- [ ] Does the text admit uncertainty anywhere? If never, it's overconfident. Add one honest hedge with a reason.
